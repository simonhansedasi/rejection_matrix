# Cover Letter — Salesforce Staff Product Data Scientist (Slack)

---

The most interesting data science question I worked on last year was: given a Salesforce sales org, how much of observed win rate is attributable to rep behavior versus pipeline structure? I built an agent-based simulator to find out — 4 rep archetypes, 9 micro-actions, a 3-step lookahead decision engine — and applied OLS regression to isolate action efficacy controlling for stage, outcome, and archetype. It is, in its essentials, what a product data scientist does: define the question, build the measurement framework, and isolate signal from noise.

That project drew on instincts I bring to any quantitative problem. My MSc in Geophysical Data Science trained me in a field where ground truth is hard to reach and measurement error is part of the answer; the methods transfer. I have applied the same framework to policy analysis (a transit equity study whose outputs were delivered to Snohomish County regional planning agencies), causal validation via enforcement record matching, and peer-reviewed publication in the Journal of Glaciology. The domain changes; the framework stays.

What draws me to this role specifically is the scale of the product question. Slack's usage data spans industries, team sizes, and collaboration patterns; identifying what is actually driving engagement versus what is merely correlated with it requires careful measurement design more than raw compute power. I have built with Apache Airflow and am currently migrating a geospatial ML pipeline to PySpark — replacing a Python-native stack with distributed feature engineering across 32,000+ training rows and MLlib model training. My Presto and Hive experience is limited; Spark is where I have been working. The harder part — knowing which metric to define, which confounders to control for, which finding is worth taking to a VP — is what I have been building.

I would welcome the opportunity to talk through the role.
